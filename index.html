Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  3. Camels travel in herds and both dromedary and Bactrian camels are social creatures.

Chameleon

  1. They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  2. Chameleons are a very unique branch of the lizard group of reptiles.
  3. Species of chameleon can be as small as 15 mm (0.59 in) or as large as 69 cm (27 in).

Cicada

  1. Cicadas are not locusts.
  2. Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
  3. More than 3,000 cicada species exist.

Panda

  1. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  2. Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  3. Bamboo is critical to their diet.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons have fought alongside humans in World War I and II.
  3. Ever heard of pigeon racing? It’s a real thing and that’s because pigeons are very fast when they take to the skies.

Teddy-Bear

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  3. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
